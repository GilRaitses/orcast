## Family summary

The uncertainty communication corpus is strong and directly relevant. The "Why Authors Don't Visualize Uncertainty" paper (1908.01697) documents — from a survey of 90 visualization practitioners — that uncertainty is systematically suppressed in maps and data products even when authors know it exists, establishing that epistemic opacity is a design failure mode, not an accident. The AI-assisted decision making papers (2001.02114, 2010.07938) demonstrate empirically that confidence displays from AI systems shape human trust in ways that can be systematically miscalibrated: when AI confidence is displayed without explanation of its basis, users either overtrust or undertrust in ways that reduce decision accuracy. The progressive disclosure paper (1811.02164) provides UX evidence that incrementally exposing the basis of algorithmic decisions improves user understanding and trust calibration. The species distribution modeling review (2107.10400) and the sample bias correction paper (2103.07107) confirm that SDM outputs are routinely presented as confident maps without surfacing the bias-correction assumptions, mirroring the orcast problem precisely. The "designing transparency cues" paper (2108.02325) shows that transparency features in information products measurably increase trust calibration when they surface source reliability — the same principle as orcast's integrity conditions visible alongside confidence.

**Verdict: Supported** — overtrust when uncertainty is omitted is empirically demonstrated in AI-assisted decision making. Primary citations: 2001.02114 (AI confidence and trust calibration), 2010.07938 (cognitive bias in AI-assisted decisions), 1908.01697 (uncertainty suppression in visualizations), 2107.10400 (SDM confidence presentation).
