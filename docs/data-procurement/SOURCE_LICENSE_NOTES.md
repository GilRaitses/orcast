# ORCAST Source License and Access Notes

## Public Agency / Government Sources

These are the safest first-wave sources, subject to attribution and no-endorsement/no-warranty language:

- NOAA CO-OPS water level, currents, wind, pressure, and temperature APIs.
- NOAA NDBC weather and sea-state files.
- NOAA Marine Cadastre AIS bulk data and NOAA PMEL ERDDAP AIS tables.
- NOAA CUSP shoreline.
- NOAA ETOPO1 bathymetry.
- NOAA Marine Protected Areas Inventory.
- Canada CPCAD and BC data where covered by the Open Government Licence.
- WSDOT and BC ferry GIS services.
- WA DNR kelp and habitat layers, subject to state GIS terms.

## Public But Dataset-Specific Licenses

- OBIS: dataset-specific Creative Commons licenses; preserve dataset citation/license metadata.
- iNaturalist: observation and media licenses vary; preserve license fields and avoid bulk scraping through app API.

## Public-Visible But Permission-Sensitive

- OrcaHello detections API: read endpoints are public, but license/retention terms should be confirmed with Orcasound/AI4Orcas before bulk redistribution. Moderator identifiers/comments may be personal data.
- Orca Network public reports: public pages exist, but structured reuse should be requested.
- Center for Whale Research encounter records: strong validation value, but structured use should be requested.
- Happywhale: account and permission likely needed for research export/API.

## Avoid For Now

- Commercial AIS vendors: NOAA AIS is adequate for first pass.
- Scraping kayak/whale-watch route maps: route maps are likely copyrighted and not stable GIS.
