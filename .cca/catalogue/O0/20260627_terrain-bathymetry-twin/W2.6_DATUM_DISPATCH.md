## Operator report

Islands are not aligned with sea level (or the water is not aligned to sea level);
the coastline looks wrong, detached, or floating.

## Verified root cause (grounded, do not re-derive)

The CUDEM tileset is correct: glTF Y = NAVD88 elevation in metres, range
roughly -376..+734 m. The bug is in the viewer frame reconciliation:

1. `web/lib/scene/tiles/useTilesLayer.ts` uniformly fits the tileset to
   `SCENE_WIDTH`, then recenters the runtime **bounding-sphere center** to world
   origin. That center is the elevation midpoint (~178 m), so **NAVD88 0 m lands
   at a negative scene Y**, not at Y 0.
2. The water plane is hardcoded at `level: 0` in `SalishScene.tsx` /
   `web/lib/scene/water2/depthWater.ts`, and the terrain shoreline tint band in
   `web/lib/scene/terrain/terrainStylist.ts` keys off `abs(terrainY) == 0`. Both
   assume scene Y 0 == sea level, which is now false.
3. Secondary: the bathy/substrate overlay uses legacy `HEIGHT_SCALE = 0.04`
   (`web/lib/sceneIntent.ts`) instead of the live fit scale
   `worldUnitsPerMeter` (~0.003), making it ~12x too tall. `TerrainStylistRig`
   also defaults `worldUnitsPerMeter` to 0.0024 instead of the live fit value.

## Mandate (diffs-only, no commit)

1. After the uniform fit, apply a vertical offset so **NAVD88 0 m maps to scene
   Y 0** (use the tileset's known elevation reference / root bounding-volume
   transform, not the sphere center). Keep the horizontal fit unchanged.
2. Drive `makeWater2({ level })` and the foam/column reference from that same
   sea-level reference, not a hardcoded 0.
3. Replace `HEIGHT_SCALE` in the bathy/substrate projector and the terrain
   stylist `worldUnitsPerMeter` with the live fit scale
   (`fitRadius / geoRadiusMeters(TILESET_BOUNDS)`), so overlays and the shore
   band sit at the corrected sea level.
4. Keep edits surgical. `SalishScene.tsx` is the convergence file: a single
   editor. Do not touch the researcher-gate, planner, or any console component.
