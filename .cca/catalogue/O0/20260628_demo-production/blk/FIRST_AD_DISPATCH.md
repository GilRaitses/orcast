# Stage 1, BLK, First-AD dispatch

Director of DBLK: dispatch one First AD per live beat (A1, A2, A3, A4, A5, A6, B1, B2, B3),
in parallel, disjoint scope. Slide beats are not blocked.

## Hydration (in order)

1. `../PROGRAM.md` (honesty locks, discipline invariants).
2. `WAVESET_CHARTER.md` (method, your beat's moving-parts seed).
3. `../BEAT_SET.md` (your beat's Say / Show / Evidence / Honesty).
4. The built surfaces your beat exercises (cite file + symbol).
5. `../set/READINESS.md` (confirm your beat's prerequisites are READY).

## Mandate

- Decompose your beat's Show into ordered moving parts.
- Clear each part in isolation with a Read-examined runtime observation (quote the real
  rendered marker or API turn, plus the built file and symbol). Then clear the parts
  together in the running app, in beat order.
- Confirm the Honesty caption is presentable: the real figure, the gate caveat, the
  modeled/estimate label, and (for A-side beats) that no reviewer-only copy is on screen.
- Write `findings/BLK-<beat>.md`: each part CLEARED or BLOCKED with a numbered defect,
  file, and symbol.

## Hard limits

- No capture (that is CAM). No approval (that is SCR).
- Do not edit product code. A BLOCKED part is a numbered defect, not a fix.
- Stay inside your beat's surfaces.

## Escalation catch

If a part cannot be cleared or blocked from a real observation, escalate to the Director
with what you observed. Do not fake a clear, do not block on the human, do not assume.

## Return

Return your beat's CLEARED/BLOCKED card. The Director assembles `BLK-ROLLUP.md` and
decides which beats advance to CAM.
