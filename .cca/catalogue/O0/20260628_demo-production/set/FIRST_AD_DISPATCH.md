# Stage 0, SET, First-AD dispatch

Director of DSET: dispatch one First AD per prerequisite row in `WAVESET_CHARTER.md`, in
parallel, disjoint scope. Give each First AD this contract.

## Hydration (each First AD reads, in order)

1. `../PROGRAM.md` (role model, honesty locks, discipline invariants).
2. `WAVESET_CHARTER.md` (this stage; find your prerequisite row).
3. `../BEAT_SET.md` (the beats your prerequisite serves).
4. The built surface you exercise (the route, the API, the scene).

## Mandate (per First AD)

- Stand the one prerequisite up. For long-lived processes, start them and record the port
  and pid.
- Verify with a Read-examined runtime check exactly as named in your row. Open the real
  health body, the real authenticated response, or the real rendered frame and read it.
  READY is never asserted from a launch command alone.
- Write `findings/SET-<id>.md`: READY / NOT READY, the check you Read-examined (quote the
  observed value, e.g. the HTTP status and the rendered marker), ports/pids, and for NOT
  READY the exact gap plus an operator-actionable fix.

## Hard limits

- No capture, no scoring, no approval. Those belong to CAM and SCR.
- Do not edit product code. A broken prerequisite is a NOT READY card, not a fix.
- Touch only your prerequisite's files and processes. Disjoint scope keeps the wave safe.

## Escalation catch

If you cannot make a clean READY/NOT READY determination from a real runtime observation,
stop and escalate to the Director with what you observed and the blocker. Do not fake a
result, do not block on the human, do not assume.

## Return

Return your `SET-<id>.md` verdict (READY / NOT READY), the Read-examined evidence, and any
ports/pids. The Director assembles `READINESS.md` and confirms the advance gate.
